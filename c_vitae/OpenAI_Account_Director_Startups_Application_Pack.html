Memo Example

This is a suggested follow-up artifact, not something to attach blindly unless the application asks for extra material. Use it after a recruiter screen or when a warm referrer asks for a crisp note.

How OpenAI Can Win AI-Native Startups In Europe

Thesis: the best AI startups do not want a vendor. They want a technical and strategic partner who helps them ship faster, avoid dead ends, and build defensible products on frontier models.

1. Find them before they are obvious

Track founder-quality signals: unusual product velocity, model-native workflows, technical communities, early revenue without a full sales function, and founders already hacking with OpenAI tools.

2. Segment by build pattern

Do not treat all startups as "AI startups." Segment by pattern: agentic workflow companies, vertical SaaS copilots, infrastructure/devtools, data-rich regulated workflows, creative tools, and consumer-native AI.

3. Earn technical credibility quickly

Founders trust people who understand latency, evals, prompt/workflow design, cost curves, failure modes, and product-market timing. GTM should sound technical without pretending to be engineering.

90-Day View For The Role

  1. Map the ecosystem: build a London/Europe startup account map across seed to Series B, with founders, investors, accelerators, and technical communities ranked by relevance to OpenAI usage.
  2. Define a founder conversation pattern: ask what they are building, where model constraints shape product, where cost/latency/reliability matter, and what would make OpenAI more useful to their roadmap.
  3. Create account plans: separate "growth now" accounts from "strategic future" accounts; tie each to usage potential, executive relationships, product blockers, and next technical engagement.
  4. Build feedback loops: synthesize recurring founder pain into product, support, solutions architecture, and marketing insights.
  5. Show up in the ecosystem: founder dinners, office hours with VC funds, technical workshops, hack days, and private product briefings for the builders most likely to compound.

Your Personal Edge In The Memo

Close the memo with one sentence tying it back to you:

My edge is that I can sit with a founder as a GTM partner, investor-type pattern matcher, and builder who has actually shipped model-driven products.
